## 🌍 Introduction

Every year on **May 1st**, the world comes together to celebrate **International Workers’ Day**, also known as **Labour Day**. This day is more than just a public holiday—it is a powerful reminder of the struggles, sacrifices, and achievements of workers across the globe.

From construction sites to tech industries, from farmers in rural fields to developers behind screens—workers are the backbone of every economy and society.

---

## 🛠️ Why International Workers’ Day Matters

International Workers’ Day is a tribute to the dignity of labor and the rights of workers. It highlights:

- Fair wages and safe working conditions  
- Respect and recognition for all professions  
- Workers’ rights and social justice  
- The importance of unity among workers  

This day reminds us that progress is built on the hard work and dedication of millions of people.

---

## 📜 A Brief History

The origin of International Workers’ Day dates back to the **Haymarket Affair (1886) in Chicago**, where workers protested for an **8-hour workday**. Their sacrifice became a symbol of labor rights movements worldwide.

Today, May Day is observed in many countries as a celebration of workers’ contributions and a call for continued progress.
